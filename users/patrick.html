About Me

Hi, this is my page, but you likely already knew that. I'm currently an undergraduate student at Purdue University in West Lafayette, Indiana. I am majoring in Computer Science, and hope to obtain minors in Mathematics and Physics. My interests include *NIX like systems, cryptography and cryptographic systems, machine learning algorithms and applications, and applied cybersecurity. I hope to become more involved in CERIAS here at Purdue, and I hope to attend Graduate school after I obtain my B.Sc.

Relevant Work Experience

I've worked at IvyHill Technologies in New Jersey as an interning applied security reseacher and then as a junior Drupal developer. I plan on continuing my work there over the Summer of 2018.

Computer Science Skills

As of Fall 2017, I know basic Python, Java, PyCrypto (a Python2 module that supplies cryptographic primitives), Drupal, Linux, LaTeX, and have a working knowledge of PGP/GPG (see my key below!).

Projects

The first project I ever did was Cryptosnake, a very small Python script that encrypted a user's string using AES in CBC mode with PBKDF2. I started creating a larger, more ambitious version of it that would fix some issues with the original version (such as not operating on bytes), but I haven't been working on it since school started. I also helped create a website for locating nearby charities at HackBCAIII alongside a group of 5 other people in 24 hours. I worked alongside Chris and Aditya at HackMIT to create ADBlockAR, an application that leverages Microsoft's HoloLens to remove advertisements in real life.
